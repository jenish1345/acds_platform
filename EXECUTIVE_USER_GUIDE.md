## Understanding Alerts

### Alert Severity Levels

**CRITICAL (Red)**
- Immediate executive attention required
- High financial or operational impact
- Potential for significant business disruption
- Action needed within 24-48 hours

**WARNING (Amber)**
- Requires management attention
- Medium impact on operations
- Trending toward critical if unaddressed
- Action needed within 1-2 weeks

**HEALTHY (Green)**
- Informational only
- Low or no immediate impact
- Monitoring recommended
- No urgent action required

### Alert Information

Each alert displays:
- **Title**: Brief description of the issue
- **Department**: Affected business unit
- **Impact Level**: High, Medium, or Low
- **Detection Date**: When the system identified the issue
- **Description**: Detailed explanation
- **Affected Metrics**: KPIs impacted by this issue

## Business Impact Assessment

### Financial Impact

**Estimated Impact**
- Most likely financial consequence

**Impact Range**
- Minimum to maximum potential impact
- Accounts for uncertainty

**Affected Revenue**

### Probability & Timeframe

**Probability Percentage**
- Likelihood of impact occurring
- Based on historical patterns
- Risk-adjusted projections

**Timeframe**
- When impact will materialize
- Planning horizon
- Urgency indicator

## Strategic Recommendations

### Understanding Recommendations

Each recommendation includes:

**Title & Description**
- Clear action item
- Detailed implementation approach
- Strategic rationale

**Priority Level**
- Critical: Immediate action required
- High: Action within 2 weeks
- Medium: Action within 1 month
- Low: Action within quarter

**Expected Outcome**
- Quantified benefits
- Success metrics
- Value proposition

**Effort Level**
- Low: < 2 weeks, minimal resources
- Medium: 2-8 weeks, moderate resources
- High: > 8 weeks, significant resources

**Timeline**
- Implementation duration
- Milestone planning
- Deadline expectations

**Owner**
- Accountable executive
- Primary stakeholder
- Escalation point

### Taking Action

1. **Review** all recommendations for an alert
2. **Prioritize** based on impact and effort
3. **Assign** to designated owner (if not already assigned)
4. **Monitor** implementation progress
5. **Track** outcomes against expectations

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often is data updated?**
A: Real-time for critical alerts, hourly for KPIs, daily for comprehensive analysis.

**Q: Can I customize the dashboard?**
A: Future releases will include customization. Current version optimized for executive needs.

**Q: Who has access to this system?**
A: Role-based access: Executives see all data, Managers see department data, Analysts see operational data.

**Q: How are alerts generated?**
A: AI-powered algorithms analyze operational data, financial metrics, and external factors to identify anomalies and risks.

**Q: What should I do about a Critical alert?**
A: Review Root Cause Analysis, assess Business Impact, review Recommendations, assign owner, set timeline, monitor progress.

**Q: Can I export data for presentations?**
A: Yes, use the Executive Report export function for board-ready PDFs.

**Q: How accurate are the financial impact estimates?**
A: Based on historical data and statistical modeling. Range provided to account for uncertainty. Confidence levels indicated.

**Q: What if I disagree with a recommendation?**
A: Recommendations are AI-generated suggestions. Executive judgment always takes precedence. Use as decision support, not mandate.
